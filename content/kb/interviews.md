# Motivation

Understand why are you interviewing. Have your goals clean, they will guide you
to be consistent and focused in the preparation.

Some usual motivations are:

- Better salary
- New learning
- Better work

# Shortlisting

Things that matter for getting shortlisted are:

- **Early application** - Recruiters stop looking into application once they find few matching profiles.
- **Referral** - It increases the chances a huge factor.
- **OSS Contributions** - It can be a gamechanger at times. People do reach out by checking OSS contributions.
- **Well read resume** - The resume should be decent, it should have impact clearly mentioned.
- **Matching profile** - Meeting basis requirements of the roles is generally nonnegotiable.

# Increasing the Chances of Conversion

- Solid DSA - decent ranks in competitive coding contests.
- Meaningful contributions to OSS.
  - Multiple commits over long period.
  - Meaningful improvements.
  - PR reviews.
  - Architectural changes.
- Speaking at conferences.
- Blogs.
- Self published OSS projects with high reach.
